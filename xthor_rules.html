Encoding rules


For MULTi releases, the original language of the film must be present.
The minimum encoding parameters allowed are those described in paragraph 3.10 here 2011_FR_X264 and in general it is forbidden to make rls of lower quality than release scenes, the parameters must be written in the nfo
Subtitles in pgs and vobsub format are not authorized for encodings (except 3d or if they are optional), encodings should be cropped and ocr should be done for any useful subtitles on the original source, OCR should be checked and corrected if there are any errors, subtitles in a separate file are only tolerated to enable Dolby Vision.

DVDRiP / BDRiP / BRRiP
  • XviD and x264 codec only allowed. DivX tolerated in some cases
  • Size of 700 MB for MP3 (~ 5%) and 1.37 GB for AC3 for movies under 2 hours. Size of 1.37 GB for MP3 and 2.05 GB for AC3 for movies longer than 2 hours.
  • The video/audio weight ratio should be consistent, the audio should not be heavier than the video
  • Sources of encodes must be cited.
  • The film resolution must be a multiple of 16 (Ex. 704 x 304 and 608 x 336) and have an AR (Aspect Ratio) of 1.33 for 4/3 and 1.78, 1.85 or 2.35 for 16/ 9 and respect the original source AR.
  • Movies with a video bitrate lower than 750 Kbps are prohibited.
  • AC3 films must be offered with a maximum bitrate of 448kbps. AC3 640 kbps is prohibited.

BluRay
  • Movies must be encoded with x264 or x265 codec in MKV format (mp4 is allowed only for Dolby Vision)
  • Sources of encodes must be cited.
  • Minimum bitrate for 720p is 4000 Kbps (2000 for anime). The minimum bitrate for 1080p is 8000 Kbps (4500 for anime).
  • The resolution of the film must be a multiple of 16 and have an AR (Aspect Ratio) of 1.78, 1.85 or 2.35/2.40 and respect the original AR of the source.
  • Minimum audio bitrate of 384 Kbps (unless the source is of lower quality), the AAC codec is forbidden but tolerated for anime, a lower bitrate is also tolerated for anime

x265
  • Releases must not be of lower quality than scene releases https://scenerules.org/n .html?id=2020_X265.nfo

WEB-DL and WEBRip
  • The bitrate rules do not apply to WEB-DL but apply to WEBRip, it is tolerated to re-encode a WEB-DL in sd but not in 720p if the bitrate of the source is too low.

Download Rules

  • Your ratio should never drop below 0.8.
  • Having a low ratio can lead to a ban from your account.
  • The Multi Tracker (Having multiple announce URLs for a Torrent) is prohibited. In order to avoid this, we advise you to read the FAQ on this subject.

Minimum required ratio

  • The minimum ratio requested on the site is 0.8. Below, it is strongly advised to put your downloaded torrents back in seed in order to earn bonus points which will allow you to raise your ratio.
  • When your ratio drops below 0.8, you will receive an automatic warning and you will no longer be able to download.
  • From the warning, you have 21 days to go back above 0.8. After this time, your account will be banned due to a low ratio.
  • In order to quickly raise your ratio when it has fallen below 0.8, we advise you to read the FAQ on this subject.

Rules on the Hit&Run

  • You must stay in seed for a minimum of 24 hours on each downloaded torrent. VIPs are immune to H&R and therefore do not have to stay in seed
  • If the seeding time is not respected, then you must have obtained a minimum ratio of 0.8 on the torrent.
  • If neither of these two goals are met after 10 days after downloading the torrent, you will be notified for Hit & Run.
  • Clicking on your nickname will allow you to see your download history, clicking on 'Torrents Completed', and see the seed time of each torrent.
  • The staff remains available to correct any system failure and reserves the right to withdraw an H&R if warranted.
  • After 15 H&R, your account will be banned. To reduce your number of H&R, we advise you to read the FAQ on this subject.
